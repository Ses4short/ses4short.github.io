About the Lab

MAIDES Lab

The MAIDES Lab — Mathematics, AI, Disease, Ecology, and Systems — is dedicated to developing rigorous, interpretable, and practical analytical systems for health and ecological risk. The lab sits at the intersection of applied mathematics, epidemiology, ecology, and data science.

The central question driving all work here: how can mathematically rigorous and data-informed models improve our understanding of complex biological systems, and support better decisions under uncertainty? Models are not abstract endpoints — they are tools for integrating evidence, testing hypotheses, quantifying uncertainty, evaluating interventions, and generating actionable insight.

The lab operates through a structured five-stage pipeline: data integration → mechanistic modelling → statistical inference and machine learning → decision analysis and optimisation → translation into forecasting tools and policy-relevant outputs.

Core research themes

What We Work On

Climate-Driven Disease Forecasting

Transmission models where epidemiological dynamics depend on climatic and environmental drivers. Probabilistic forecasts with uncertainty quantification and early-warning signal generation.

One Health, Ecology & AMR

Mathematical and ecological models for systems where human, animal, and environmental processes interact — antimicrobial resistance, zoonotic spillover, and cross-sector transmission.

Environmental Health & Chronic Disease

Time-series analysis, state-space models, and causal inference for long-term cumulative health burdens shaped by environmental exposures and service-system delays.

Dynamic Intervention Design

Optimal control methods and scenario simulation to evaluate intervention timing, intensity, and resource allocation — converting model results into strategic recommendations.

Explainable AI & Hybrid Models

Lattice-theoretic frameworks using Formal Concept Analysis for ensemble methods. Hybrid models integrating mechanistic structure with machine learning — interpretable and policy-communicable.
