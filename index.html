About the Lab

The MAIDES Lab — Mathematics, AI, Disease, Ecology, and Systems — is dedicated to developing rigorous, interpretable, and practical analytical systems for health and ecological risk. The lab sits at the intersection of applied mathematics, epidemiology, ecology, and data science.

The research question driving all work here is: how can mathematically rigorous and data-informed models improve our understanding of complex biological and health systems, and support better decisions under uncertainty? Mathematical models are not treated as abstract endpoints — they are tools for integrating evidence, testing hypotheses, quantifying uncertainty, evaluating interventions, and generating actionable insight.

The lab operates through a structured five-stage pipeline: data integration, mechanistic and ecological modelling, statistical inference and machine learning, decision analysis and optimisation, and translation into forecasting tools and policy-relevant outputs.

Core Research Themes

Climate-Driven Disease Forecasting

Province-level and regional transmission models where key epidemiological quantities depend on climatic and environmental drivers — temperature, rainfall, humidity, mobility. Probabilistic forecasts with uncertainty quantification and early-warning signal generation.

One Health, Ecology & AMR

Mathematical and ecological models for systems where human, animal, and environmental processes interact — antimicrobial resistance, zoonotic spillover, cross-sector transmission pathways, and ecological interface dynamics.

Environmental Health & Chronic Disease

Time-series analysis, state-space models, and causal inference for long-term cumulative health burdens shaped by environmental exposures and service-system delays. Quantitative risk modelling for earlier detection and prevention.

Dynamic Intervention Design

Optimal control methods, dynamic optimisation, and scenario simulation to evaluate intervention timing, intensity, and resource allocation. Converting model results into strategic recommendations for health systems.

Explainable AI & Hybrid Models

Lattice-theoretic frameworks using Formal Concept Analysis (FCA) to generate structural, hierarchical explanations for ensemble methods. Hybrid models integrating mechanistic structure with machine learning — interpretable and communicable to both scientific and policy audiences.
