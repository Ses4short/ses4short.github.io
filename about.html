Professional Bio

I am an applied mathematician and data scientist with research interests spanning mathematical modelling, machine learning, explainable AI, epidemiological modelling, and ecological systems. My work combines mechanistic modelling, predictive analytics, and interpretable computational methods to address problems in health, agriculture, and environmental systems.

I am currently completing two concurrent doctoral programs: a PhD in Applied Mathematics at York University (Toronto, Canada), where my thesis models ecological dynamics under climate change and fishing pressure in Canadian marine ecosystems; and a PhD in Data Science at Prince of Songkla University (Thailand), where my thesis advances predictive modelling and explainable machine learning in the oil palm industry.

I have experience teaching mathematics, applied mathematics, and data science at both undergraduate and graduate levels, including co-instruction of graduate courses at the University of Toronto.
