Research

Mathematical modelling, machine learning, and decision-support tools for health, ecological, and environmental systems.

Research Overview

My research develops mathematical and data-driven models to study complex biological, ecological, and public health systems. I work at the intersection of applied mathematics, epidemiology, ecology, data science, and machine learning, with a focus on building models that are rigorous, interpretable, and useful for decision-making.

A central theme across my work is the integration of mechanistic modelling with modern computational methods, including ordinary differential equation models, statistical learning, forecasting, uncertainty analysis, explainable AI, and simulation-based approaches.

Theme 1

Mathematical Epidemiology & Disease Modelling

This work develops and analyses mathematical models for infectious disease transmission, public health risk, and outbreak response. It includes compartmental epidemic models, risk-stratified population models, vaccination dynamics, limited-data forecasting, and model-based evaluation of disease control strategies.

Applications span rotavirus, COVID-19, hand-foot-and-mouth disease, mpox, and other emerging infectious disease settings — combining analytical methods, numerical simulation, and data-driven approaches to support public health interpretation and preparedness.

Theme 2

Ecological and Climate-Health Systems

This research studies how environmental change, climate variability, and human activity shape ecological systems and population dynamics. A major part of this work focuses on aquatic ecosystems, fisheries, and the interaction between climate stressors and biological processes.

My doctoral research develops stage-structured mechanistic models to estimate maturation, mortality, and recruitment parameters for economically important fish species. This work supports broader questions around climate adaptation, fisheries management, and the long-term resilience of ecological and human systems.

Theme 3

AI, Data Science & Forecasting

I use machine learning, statistical modelling, and time-series methods to detect patterns, generate forecasts, and extract insight from complex datasets. This work is especially interested in models that are not only predictive, but also interpretable and useful for scientific understanding.

Applications include public health surveillance, environmental risk, agricultural systems, fisheries, and social-media-derived signals related to ecological and health-related change.

Theme 4

Explainable AI & Hybrid Modelling

A key strand of my research focuses on explainable and hybrid modelling frameworks. I develop approaches that combine mechanistic structure with machine learning, aiming to make complex models more transparent, auditable, and scientifically meaningful.

One example is my work using Formal Concept Analysis to provide lattice-based explanations for ensemble learning methods such as XGBoost and Gradient Boosting. This framework helps organise feature relationships and model decision structures in a way that supports interpretation beyond standard local explanation methods.

Theme 5

Decision Modelling & Policy-Relevant Analysis

Across my research, I am interested in how models can support better decisions under uncertainty. This includes evaluating intervention strategies, comparing policy scenarios, quantifying uncertainty, and translating model outputs into practical recommendations.

This work connects mathematical modelling with real-world decision needs in public health, ecological management, environmental planning, and resource allocation.
